My Biggest Flaws

Wednesday, July 9th, 2025

Mental health is still taboo for many men, even in today's supposedly open culture. We hide our wounds, downplay our worth, and power through pain because we think vulnerability is weakness. But it isn't. This piece is an honest reflection on what it means to face yourself fully—not with shame, but with grace.

For a lot of my life I've been told I'm smart, I believe that I am to some extent too. But intelligence requires balance. I've pleaded and searched for clarity in so many situations, but instead of accepting what I saw clearly, I acted on emotion. Choosing to analyze the situation and see it from every angle, see it for what is, after things are over. I didn't act in the moment when the clarity came thus creating a tug of war of what it was, what it could've been and what it actually was.

The Core Flaw

I know who I am — I just haven't fully accepted it. So I keep giving more than I receive, punishing myself for my past, and hoping someone will hand me the peace I refuse to give myself. The root of this flaw comes from a lack of trust. I don't trust myself enough to accept myself and the things in my life.

You can't carry all of it yourself. That's what your friends and loved ones are for. You need people who can help you carry the burden. Shared joy is twice the joy and shared sorrow is half the sorrow as they say.

Grace is a part of love and patience in action. These are good things to give yourself, it isn't just for others it's for yourself too. You wouldn't be this hard on someone else, you wouldn't even treat someone else the way you treat yourself. When you have no grace for yourself and you don't reach out to anyone for anything or about what's going on, you're eventually going to build concrete emotional walls.

Building a solid foundation goes back to my first point regarding my own flaws which I'm sure you and others deal with. It's trusting your own clarity and intuition the first time. It's more than just doing what's right for you in that moment, it's about doing what's right for you now and what is also going to help you later on down the line.

"Now I know: I am not here to earn love. I am here to embody it — with boundaries, with clarity, and finally, with grace for myself."

Jonny Terrero

Aspiring Renaissance Man

My journey hasn't been a straight line. For eight years, I battled depression and the flaw of giving more than I received—punishing myself for the past and refusing to accept help. I learned the hard way that intelligence without self-acceptance is just noise.

Today, I channel that experience into HeartWire. I treat the body as a system that deserves both engineering precision and human grace. My goal is to embody love with boundaries and clarity, using code and chemistry to help others build the foundation I had to fight for.
